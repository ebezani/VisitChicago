City of the Big Shoulders

Chicago has a rich history dating back to 1673. This web page will highlight the top ten years that mark the history of Chicago. The information I have gathered below is from the Chicago Public Library(CPL). If you are interested in learning more about the detailed history of Chicago, please visit the CPLwebsite, which offers additional information.

The National Geographic channel has a three-minute video that highlights the historical spots of Chicago. If you are interested in watching the video, you can unmute the sound.

1673 – The first written history about Chicago
The journals of European explorer Marquette and Joliet become the first written history of Chicago
1837 – Chicago incorporated as a city
1837 was the starting point for a fast-growing city in the Midwest of the United States.
1871 – The Great Chicago Fire
October 8, 1871, was a devastating year for the city of Chicago. The fire killed approximately 300 people, destroyed roughly 3.3 square miles, and left over 100,000 residents homeless.
1900 – Flow of the Chicago River reversed
The reversal of the Chicago River was monumental because of the engineering effort it to in addition to preventing waterborne diseases that could have resulted from sewage contamination.
1930 – The Shedd Aquarium Opened
Shedd Aquarium is an indoor public aquarium, which was the largest indoor facility in the world. You can find additional information about the Shedd Aquarium on the Activities page.
1945 – Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) created
CTA is a public transportation system that commutes Chicago residents, surrounding suburb residents, and visitors to get from one location to another with ease. Please refer to the Transportation page for further details on how you can utilize the services as a visitor.
1973 - Sears Tower ( Now known as Willis Tower) Completed
The Willis Tower was the tallest building in the world until 1998. It is a 108- story with 1, 450-foot skyscraper in Chicago. Visit the Activities page for additional information.
1979 – Airline Crash
American Airlines Flight 191 crashed on May 25, 1979, when it lost an engine after taking off from the O’Hare airport.
1995 – Deadly Heat Wave
There were over 700 heat-related deaths reported in five days. 1915, 1916, and 1955 were also other years were Chicago experienced heatwave that caused many deaths.
2008 - President-elect Barack Obama’s acceptance speech
At the time, President-elect Barack Obama made his acceptance speech in Grant Park. He represented the 13th district in the Illinois Senate from 1997 until 2004. President Obama and served as a U.S senator from Illinois from 2005 to 2008.
